# Value Equation — Detailed Component Reference

This document provides the full scoring rubrics for each component of Hormozi's Value Equation. The SKILL.md file references this — read it when you need the detailed criteria for scoring.

## The Equation

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

The numerator is what the buyer *wants* and *believes*. The denominator is what stands in the way. To increase value, either raise the numerator or shrink the denominator.

---

## 1. Dream Outcome

**Question**: How desirable is the end result for the target audience?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1–3   | Incremental improvement. Nice to have but not life-changing. |
| 4–6   | Meaningful improvement. Solves a real problem but not transformative. |
| 7–8   | Significant transformation. Changes how they work or live day-to-day. |
| 9–10  | Dream result. The outcome they fantasise about but thought was out of reach. |

### Scoring Criteria

Read the offer definition and target avatar. Ask yourself:

- **What is the promised result?** Is it specific and vivid, or vague and generic? "Lose weight" scores lower than "Fit into your wedding dress in 8 weeks and feel confident walking down the aisle."
- **How closely does it match what the target audience desperately wants?** The closer the match to an existing burning desire, the higher the score. Creating new desire is harder than matching existing desire.
- **Is the outcome identity-level or task-level?** Identity-level outcomes ("become a published author") score higher than task-level outcomes ("write 1,000 words a day") because they tap into deeper motivation.
- **Would the buyer brag about this result?** If the outcome is something the buyer would tell friends about, it scores higher.

### Common Pitfalls

- Vague outcomes ("improve your business") → score 3–4
- Feature-focused language instead of outcome-focused → score lower
- Outcomes that matter to the creator but not the buyer → score lower
- Multiple outcomes diluting the message → score the strongest one

## 2. Perceived Likelihood

**Question**: How likely does the buyer think they will actually achieve the dream outcome?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1–3   | Sceptical. No proof, no process, no reason to believe. |
| 4–6   | Plausible. Some evidence or logical reasoning but not compelling. |
| 7–8   | Confident. Clear methodology, social proof, or demonstrated results. |
| 9–10  | Near-certain. Overwhelming proof. The process is proven and transparent. |

### Scoring Criteria

- **Does the offer include a clear methodology?** A named process or framework ("The 5-Step Sleep Reset Protocol") increases confidence. The buyer wants to see the path from A to B.
- **Is there social proof?** Case studies, testimonials, before/after results, logos of known clients. More proof = higher score.
- **Is the process transparent?** Can the buyer see exactly what they will do and what will happen? Transparency builds trust. Black boxes score lower.
- **Does it feel achievable for *this specific avatar*?** A process that works for experts but intimidates beginners scores lower for a beginner audience. The buyer must believe *they* can do it, not just that *someone* can.
- **Is there a guarantee or risk reversal?** Money-back guarantees, free trials, or "keep the bonuses even if you cancel" reduce perceived risk and increase likelihood.

### Common Pitfalls

- No proof or methodology → score 2–3
- Proof that doesn't match the target avatar → score lower
- Overuse of hype language without substance → actually decreases perceived likelihood
- Too-good-to-be-true claims without evidence → score 2–3

---

## 3. Time Delay

**Question**: How quickly does the buyer see results?

**IMPORTANT**: This score is INVERTED in the equation (it sits in the denominator). A HIGH score means FAST results, which INCREASES value. When scoring, 10 = instant results (best), 1 = takes years (worst).

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1–3   | Months to years before any meaningful result. |
| 4–6   | Weeks. Results are visible within 2–4 weeks. |
| 7–8   | Days. First results within the first week. |
| 9–10  | Hours to immediate. The buyer sees results the same day. |

### Scoring Criteria

- **When does the buyer see their first tangible result?** Not when the full transformation is complete, but when they get the first proof that it is working. Quick wins matter enormously.
- **Are there built-in quick wins?** A programme that delivers a small result on day one and builds toward the big result over weeks scores higher than one where nothing happens until week six.
- **Is the timeline explicit?** "You'll see your first result by Friday" scores higher than "results vary." Specificity builds confidence.
- **Is the perceived delay shorter than the actual delay?** Sometimes restructuring the delivery to front-load a visible quick win can improve this score without changing the total duration.

### Common Pitfalls

- No mention of timeline → buyer assumes it's slow → score 3–4
- Only describing the end result, not the journey → score lower
- "Results may vary" disclaimers → reduce score
- Long onboarding before any value delivery → score lower

---

## 4. Effort & Sacrifice

**Question**: How easy is it for the buyer?

**IMPORTANT**: This score is also INVERTED (denominator). A HIGH score means LOW effort, which INCREASES value.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1–3   | Requires significant time, money, skills, or lifestyle changes. |
| 4–6   | Moderate effort. Requires some learning and dedication. |
| 7–8   | Low effort. The process is streamlined and most of the work is done for them. |
| 9–10  | Almost effortless. Done-for-you or trivially easy to execute. |

### Scoring Criteria

- **What does the buyer have to give up or invest (beyond money)?** Time, energy, existing habits, comfort, relationships, identity. The less they sacrifice, the higher the score.
- **What skills does the buyer need that they don't currently have?** If the process requires learning new tools, concepts, or behaviours, that's effort. Pre-built templates, done-for-you elements, and guided processes reduce this.
- **How much of the work is done for them?** "Done-for-you" scores 9–10. "Done-with-you" scores 6–8. "Do-it-yourself with instructions" scores 3–5. "Figure it out" scores 1–2.
- **Does the buyer need to change their identity or habits?** Identity-level change ("become a morning person") requires more sacrifice than behaviour-level change ("set an alarm"). Score accordingly.

### Common Pitfalls

- Assuming the buyer knows things they don't → underestimating effort
- Listing many steps or prerequisites → score lower
- Requiring tool installation, account setup, or configuration → score lower for non-technical audiences
- "Just follow these 47 steps" → score 2–3 regardless of how well-documented they are

## The Inversion Explained

Time Delay and Effort & Sacrifice are scored where high = good (fast results, low effort). But they sit in the denominator, so we invert before dividing:

```
Time Delay_inverted = 11 - Time Delay score
Effort_inverted     = 11 - Effort & Sacrifice score
```

This means:
- Time Delay = 8 (fast) → denominator contribution = 11 − 8 = 3 (low drag)
- Time Delay = 3 (slow) → denominator contribution = 11 − 3 = 8 (high drag)
- Effort = 9 (effortless) → denominator contribution = 11 − 9 = 2 (low drag)
- Effort = 2 (very hard) → denominator contribution = 11 − 2 = 9 (high drag)

The formula:

```
Overall = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay_inverted × Effort_inverted)
```

Cap at 10: `min(10, Overall)`.

## Interpreting the Overall Score

| Overall Score | Interpretation |
|---------------|----------------|
| 1.0–2.0       | Weak offer. The friction outweighs the promise. Needs significant rework. |
| 2.1–4.0       | Moderate offer. Has potential but at least one component is dragging it down. |
| 4.1–6.0       | Good offer. Solid fundamentals. Targeted improvements can push it higher. |
| 6.1–8.0       | Strong offer. Compelling value proposition. Fine-tuning only. |
| 8.1–10.0      | Exceptional offer. Very high perceived value. Protect what's working. |

These bands are guidelines, not gospel. Context matters — a 3.7 in a market with no competition might still win, while a 6.0 in a crowded market might not stand out.

## Improving Each Component

### Raising Dream Outcome
- Make the outcome more specific and vivid
- Connect to identity-level desires, not just task-level goals
- Use the buyer's own language (from avatar research)
- Focus on one transformative outcome rather than many incremental ones

### Raising Perceived Likelihood
- Add case studies, testimonials, or before/after results
- Name and explain your methodology
- Make the process transparent — show the buyer the path
- Add a guarantee or risk reversal
- Match proof to the specific target avatar

### Improving Time Delay (Raising the Score)
- Front-load a quick win — something visible on day one
- Break the journey into milestones with visible progress markers
- State the timeline explicitly
- Restructure delivery to surface early results

### Reducing Effort & Sacrifice (Raising the Score)
- Provide templates, checklists, or done-for-you elements
- Remove prerequisites and setup steps
- Simplify the process — fewer steps, less learning required
- Offer a guided or "done-with-you" path
- Reduce the number of tools or platforms the buyer needs to use
